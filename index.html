Tulancingo

Oficially "Tulancingo de Bravo" Second largest city of the state of Hidalgo. The name derives from the Nahuatl words “tule” and “tzintle” which mean “in or behind the reeds.”

Brief History

A city was founded in 645 BCE by the Toltecs with the name Tolancingo as part of the empire centered in Tula. During the Spanish invasion and due to the warm climate and fertile soil the land attracted a lot of spanish settlers, especially older ones. In time, the area became known as the “Retiring place of Old Conquistadors.” During the Mexican fight for independence Tulancingo was occupied by Nicolas Bravo and Guadalupe Victoria; Bravo remained here for a time, founding a newspaper called El Mosquito de Tulancingo and constructing a gunpowder factory. This would lead to “de Bravo” being added as an appendage to the city's name in 1858.

Points of Iterest in the city:

  • City Center: where you can find all types of food, the Guajolote being my personal favorite
  • "El Santo" Museum and old theatre, where you can learn addditional history of the city and watch a classic movie although not only from Mexico.
  • Huapalcalco Architectural zone, for sightseeing although you can't climb the pyramid
